Food Waste Reduction Tips

Learn practical ways to reduce food waste and save money

Smart Storage

Temperature Matters

Store fruits and vegetables at the right temperature. Keep potatoes, onions, and garlic in cool, dark places. Refrigerate berries, leafy greens, and herbs.

Examples:
  • Bananas: Room temperature until ripe, then refrigerate
  • Tomatoes: Room temperature for best flavor
  • Herbs: In water like flowers, cover with plastic bag

Proper Ventilation

Some produce needs air circulation while others need humidity. Use perforated bags for vegetables that need airflow.

Best practices:
  • Mushrooms: Paper bags, not plastic
  • Lettuce: Wrap in paper towels to absorb moisture
  • Apples: Store away from other fruits (they release ethylene)

Freezer Friendly

Many foods can be frozen to extend their life. Blanch vegetables before freezing and use proper containers to prevent freezer burn.

Freezer tips:
  • Bread: Slice before freezing for easy use
  • Bananas: Peel and freeze for smoothies
  • Herbs: Freeze in ice cube trays with oil

Meal Planning

Smart Shopping Lists

Plan your meals for the week and create a detailed shopping list. Check what you already have before shopping.

Planning steps:
  • Check your pantry and fridge first
  • Plan meals around ingredients you already have
  • Buy only what you need for planned meals

Batch Cooking

Cook larger portions and use leftovers creatively. Transform yesterday's dinner into today's lunch with simple additions.

Transformation ideas:
  • Roast chicken → Chicken salad → Chicken soup
  • Rice → Fried rice → Rice pudding
  • Vegetables → Stir-fry → Soup → Smoothie

First In, First Out

Use older items first. Organize your fridge and pantry so older items are in front and visible.

Organization tips:
  • Label containers with dates
  • Keep a "use first" section in your fridge
  • Do regular fridge cleanouts

Creative Cooking

Smoothies & Soups

Overripe fruits make excellent smoothies, and wilted vegetables are perfect for soups and stews.

Quick ideas:
  • Brown bananas → Banana bread or smoothies
  • Soft vegetables → Vegetable soup or stock
  • Herb stems → Flavor base for soups

Regrow Vegetables

Some vegetables can be regrown from scraps. Green onions, celery, and lettuce can all be regrown in water.

Regrowable vegetables:
  • Green onions: Place roots in water
  • Celery: Place base in shallow water
  • Lettuce: Place stem in water

Use Everything

Many parts of vegetables we throw away are actually edible and nutritious. Broccoli stems, beet greens, and carrot tops can all be used.

Don't waste:
  • Broccoli stems: Peel and use in stir-fries
  • Beet greens: Sauté like spinach
  • Carrot tops: Use in pesto or chimichurri

Quick Storage Reference

Freeze

  • Cooked rice
  • Soup portions
  • Grated cheese
